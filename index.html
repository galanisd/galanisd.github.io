About Me

Currently (from April 2013), I am a Software Engineer/Researcher at Institute of Language and Speech Processing working on various R&D projects. I hold a Ph.D. in Natural Language Processing (Nov. 2006 - Feb. 2012, title: "Automatic Generation of Natural Language Summaries"), an M.Sc. in Computer Science (2004-2006) and a B.Sc. in Informatics (1999 - 2004). All the aforementioned degrees were obtained in the Department of Informatics at Athens University of Economics and Business (AUEB). In the past I worked as a Software Engineer/Research assistant at AUEB in the following projects:

  • "A linear programming approach to multi-document text summarization and natural language generation from ontologies". AUEB Basic Research Funding Program (BRFP) project (04/2011-08/2012).
  • Indigo: Interaction with mobile robots that have personalities and support multimodal dialogues. European FP6-IST project (02/2007-01/2010).
  • Xenios: Human-robot interaction using speech processing, natural language generation, and computer vision. National "Information Society" project (06/2006-12/2007).

My research interests include Natural Language Generation, Semantic Web, Question Answering, Sentence Compression, Query-Focused Summarization and Sentiment Analysis.

Software

  • NaturalOWL: a natural language generator for OWL ontologies that supports English and Greek; it can be used within Protege. [Download]
  • Sentence compression software: the software of our HLT-NAACL 2010 paper. [Download]
  • Touring Machine (TM): is a personalized digital tour guide for archeological sites and museums. It is available in three editions (web, phone and augmented reality). It is based on NaturalOWL generation engine and it got the first position in the Interoperability Challenge of Imagine Cup 2011 in New York. TM was developed by George Karakatsiotis and Vangos Pterneas. Click here to visit TM's web edition.
